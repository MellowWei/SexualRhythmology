Abstract · 230 words

Problem. Sexuality is often reduced to biological function, social behavior, identity label, moral transgression, or clinical category. Such reductions leave under-theorized the structural relation among bodily sovereignty, desire, power, fantasy, subject-presence, subject-absence, and return.

Approach. Sexual Rhythmology proposes a philosophical-ontological framework organized through 25 propositions, 14+1 thematic parts, and one core axiom: a true relation has no structural power asymmetry. It treats sexual experience not as a mere act but as a bounded event in which two sovereign subjects enter a shared vibrational field, one or both subjects temporarily withdraw from ordinary self-maintenance, and return becomes ethically decisive. The framework distinguishes consensual play from structural violence, fantasy from act, love from love-as-Eros, and subject-absence from physical self-harm.

Contribution. The site contributes a sex–body–power branch to the Source-Field ecology, a MetaLogic case of Naming → Pressure → Semantic Drift → Phase Transition, and a precursor layer for Eros Love Studies. It is not a clinical sex therapy, diagnostic instrument, empirical model of all sexual behavior, or justification of violence. It is a research artifact for conceptual, ethical, phenomenological, and design-facing analysis.
